# Mastery Matters: Educational Performance Analytics Using DMAIC


## 📌 Project Overview
This Business Intelligence capstone project applied Lean Six Sigma DMAIC methodology and statistical modeling techniques to analyze factors influencing middle school academic mastery outcomes across grades 6–8. The analysis focused on identifying the primary drivers of student performance and uncovering actionable strategies to improve educational outcomes, operational efficiency, and long-term school performance metrics.
Using anonymized educational assessment data, the project leveraged Python, Excel, regression analysis, ANOVA, correlation analysis, and data visualization techniques to evaluate academic trends, subgroup performance, and predictors of mastery outcomes.

---

## 🎯 Business Problem
Persistently low mastery rates negatively impacted school accountability metrics, funding stability, and enrollment confidence. The goal of this project was to identify root causes of low performance and provide data-driven recommendations to improve academic mastery outcomes.

## 📊 Methodology
The project followed the Lean Six Sigma DMAIC framework:
* **Defined** low mastery performance as the core operational issue
* **Measured** assessment trends and DPMO accuracy metrics
* **Applied** ANOVA, correlation analysis, and regression modeling
* **Improved** and developed data-driven instructional recommendations
* **Control** strategies and long-term monitoring recommendations

---

## 🔍 Key Findings
### Literacy Was the Strongest Predictor of Student Success
Reading performance consistently emerged as the strongest predictor of ELA mastery across all grade levels.
* 6th Grade: Reading performance increased ELA mastery by 0.56 points per performance level increase (β = 0.56)
* 7th Grade: Reading performance increased ELA mastery by 0.92 points (β = 0.92)
* 8th Grade: Reading remained the strongest predictor of ELA achievement (β = 0.73)
### Literacy Directly Influenced Math Performance
Strong literacy skills also significantly improved Math outcomes.
* 6th Grade: ELA Achievement increased Math Achievement by 0.83 points (β = 0.83)
* 7th Grade: Reading increased Math Achievement by 0.65 points (β = 0.65)
* 8th Grade: Reading and ELA remained the strongest predictors of Math performance (β = 0.35 and β = 0.29 respectively)
### Correlation Analysis Revealed Strong Academic Relationships
* Reading and ELA Achievement showed extremely strong correlations:
  * 6th Grade: r ≈ .85
  * 7th Grade: r ≈ .90
  * 8th Grade: r ≈ .78
* ELA and Math Achievement also showed moderate-to-strong relationships across all grades.
### SPED Status Was a Significant Equity Concern
SPED students consistently demonstrated lower performance outcomes across subjects, particularly in Math and Reading assessments. Regression models confirmed SPED status as a negative predictor even after controlling for literacy skills.
### Question-Level Analysis Identified Instructional Gaps
DPMO analysis identified Literary Text as a key instructional gap in 6th grade:
  * Literary Text DPMO: 716,981
  * Informational Text DPMO: 660,377
  * Vocabulary DPMO: 622,642

This indicated higher rates of weak or moderate performance in Literary Text comprehension.
